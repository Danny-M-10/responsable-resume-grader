# Rule-Based vs AI-Powered Scoring Comparison

## Example Job: Safety Specialist

**Requirements:**
- COSS, CHST, ASP, CUSP, OSHA 510 certifications
- 5+ years electrical utility experience
- Team leadership experience

## Example Candidate: Jane Smith

**Profile:**
- OSHA 510 certified (1 of 5 required)
- 8 years as Safety Coordinator
- Managed teams of 12
- Extensive electrical distribution experience

---

## Rule-Based Scoring (Current)

**Score: 6.5/10**

```
Certification Match: 1/5 = 20%
Experience: 8 years (exceeds 5+ requirement)
Skills: Basic pattern matching

Final Calculation:
(0.20 × 0.30) + (1.0 × 0.10) + (0.40 × 0.25) = 6.5/10
```

**Issues:**
- Doesn't recognize 8 years Safety Coordinator ≈ equivalent to some certifications
- Can't assess quality of experience
- Misses transferable competencies

---

## AI-Powered Scoring (NEW)

**Score: 7.8/10**

```
CERTIFICATION ANALYSIS:
The candidate has 1 of 5 required certifications (OSHA 510). However,
their 8 years as a Safety Coordinator demonstrates practical competency
that partially compensates for missing certifications.

COSS certification absence is notable, but their safety coordination
role shows equivalent practical knowledge. ASP, CHST, and CUSP could
be obtained within 6 months given their experience foundation.

EXPERIENCE EVALUATION:
8 years in safety coordination with electrical distribution focus
directly aligns with role requirements. Experience EXCEEDS the 5+ year
requirement and demonstrates progressive responsibility.

Team leadership of 12-person crews exceeds typical requirements and
shows strong mentorship capability.

OVERALL ASSESSMENT:
This is a strong candidate with addressable certification gaps. Their
practical experience provides a foundation that makes the missing
certifications obtainable with employer support. The electrical
distribution expertise is directly relevant.

RECOMMENDATION:
Interview recommended. Focus questions on:
1. Timeline for obtaining missing certifications
2. Specific OSHA 510 training outcomes
3. Safety incident reduction metrics from current role

Score: 7.8/10 (strong fit with bridgeable gaps)
```

**Benefits:**
✓ Recognizes equivalent experience
✓ Assesses quality, not just presence
✓ Provides actionable recommendations
✓ Explains reasoning clearly

## When to Use Each

**Rule-Based (Free):**
- High volume initial screening
- Budget constraints
- Offline operation needed
- Simple job requirements

**AI-Powered ($0.03 each):**
- Final candidate selection
- Complex role requirements
- Nuanced evaluation needed
- Want detailed explanations

**Hybrid (Recommended):**
- Use rule-based for initial screening (1000 → 100 candidates)
- Use AI for top candidates (100 → 10 finalists)
- Total cost: ~$3 instead of ~$30
- Get best of both approaches
